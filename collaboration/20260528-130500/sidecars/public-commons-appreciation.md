# Sidecar — Public legal-data commons appreciation (seed for H-MO10)

The assay is not possible without a public, canonical, free legal-data commons. Both projects already stand on this ground; this note affirms it and proposes we build on it together and credit it loudly.

## The commons we depend on

- **Free Law Project / CourtListener** — case law, opinions, dockets, citation lookup, judges. The backbone of deterministic case-citation verification.
- **Cornell Legal Information Institute (LII)** — U.S. Code, CFR, and accessible primary law.
- **GovInfo (U.S. GPO)** — Public Laws, the Federal Register, and authoritative federal documents.

## Why it matters for the assay

Deterministic grounding (`Assay.Citation.VerifyFirst@v1`) is only as honest as the source it checks against. A free, public, canonical commons is what makes "verify-first" a public good rather than a proprietary moat — anyone can check the same citation against the same source and get the same answer.

## The proposal

1. Both projects treat the commons as the shared grounding substrate, not a vendor dependency.
2. Both projects credit it visibly in user-facing output when a verdict is sourced from it.
3. Both projects consider giving back — corrections, coverage gaps, or support — to the maintainers who keep it free.

/ The maintainers of these resources are part of the extended collaboration network this collaboration addresses. Thank you. /
